qualitatively increases. This is because
emissivity is a measure of how efficiently a

and radiated heat flux can be described by the
Stefan-Boltzmann law, which states that the
radiated heat flux \( q \) is proportional to the
fourth power of the absolute temperature \( T \)
and the emissivity \( \epsilon \):

\[

\]

where:
- \( q \) is the radiated heat flux,
- \( \epsilon \) is the emissivity,
- \( \sigma \) is the Stefan-Boltzmann constant,
- \( T \) is the absolute temperature.

From this equation, we can see that as emissivity
\( \epsilon \) increases, the radiated heat flux
\( q \) increases, assuming the temperature \( T
\) remains constant. This is because the term \(
\epsilon T^4 \) dominates the equation, and a
higher emissivity leads to a higher radiated heat
