a15 layer thickness and surface contamination, shortening the effective load path
and increasing local heating
Answer:

The assumption that the insulation is a cellular polymer with a consistent
microstructure and no significant chemical or moisture-driven degradation reduces
the ability to detect local damage before it becomes serviceable. If there are any
trapped voids or open pores that reduce the cell wall thickness and raise local
stress concentrations, those voids or defects act as micro‑notches that seed crack
growth. Similarly, if surface contamination or oil separates during transport and
handling, it can create a sustained local undervoltage and extend the overcurrent
interval, thereby increasing both heater element heating and trip thresholds. The
qualitative failure chain is therefore: a consistent, cell-based insulation
property → reduced local heating and improved thermal contact → increased contact
resistance and further local heating → elevated temperature of the relay or heater
elements → premature mechanical stripping or joint separation → loss of service
capability.
