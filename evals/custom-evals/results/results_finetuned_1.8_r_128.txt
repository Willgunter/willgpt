a5
A) The air flow is uniform across all directions
B) The heat transfer coefficient remains constant through the material and seals
C) Convection at the lower end of the duct becomes negligible compared with natural conduction into the panel
D) Pressure drop across the entire duct path stays within acceptable limits
Answer:

B

The assumption that the convective heat-transfer coefficient throughout the system remains stable and unchanged breaks down as pressure loss, fouling or seal degradation change local flow patterns so convection actually rises where it can extract more energy while also losing effectiveness elsewhere; therefore, the net heat-removal capability of the device declines because the same amount of work cannot be done by available airflow.
