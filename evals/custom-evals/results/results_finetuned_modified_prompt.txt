What failure modes follow if those assumptions break down?

Assuming constant lubricant viscosity is one of the most important assumptions for
predicting film pressure and load distribution because viscosity controls how much
fluid resists shear, which directly affects cavity formation and local wall loads.
If temperature rises relative to cold start, so does viscosity; viscosity loss
reduces viscous damping and can cause cavitation or dry-out at some contacts while
increasing shear at others.
Viscosity change also alters local shear-driven mixing, changing where oil reaches
required thicknesses and where thinning occurs.
When viscosity falls too much, contact regions get thinner and more prone to local
stress singularity or rupture
